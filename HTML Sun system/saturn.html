Size of Saturn
Saturn, with a radius of 58,232 km, is the second largest planet in our solar system - only the other gas giant, Jupiter, is larger than it. To make a trip along the planet's equator, you need to cover a distance of 365,882 kilometers! How many times larger is Saturn than Earth? The radius of Saturn is about 9.5 times the radius of our planet; in addition, the gas giant is almost 95 times more massive. To better understand the size of Saturn, imagine that 764 planets like Earth could fit inside it.
Orbit and rotation of Saturn
Each planet in the solar system takes a certain amount of time to complete one revolution around the sun and one revolution around its axis. On Earth, these periods last 365.25 days and 24 hours, respectively, but things are different on Saturn. How long is a day on Saturn? Saturn rotates once on its axis in just 10 hours and 32 minutes, making it the second shortest day among the planets in our solar system. Only Jupiter rotates faster. Due to the high speed of rotation, Saturn is flattened at the poles and expands towards the equator. How long is a year on Saturn? Like Jupiter, Saturn rotates very quickly on its axis, but it takes a long time for it to complete one rotation. around the sun. The speed with which Saturn revolves around the star is small: one year on the planet lasts 29.4571 Earth years or 10,759 Earth days.
How far away is Saturn?
How far away is Saturn? Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun. Billions of kilometers separate it from the Sun and the Earth. How far is Saturn from the Sun? The planet revolves around the Sun at a distance of about 9.5 AU. - this is 9.5 times the distance between the Earth and our star. It is equivalent to 1.486 billion kilometers.
How far is Saturn from Earth?
As Saturn and Earth travel in their orbits, the distance between them is constantly changing. During closest approach, celestial bodies are approximately 1.195 billion kilometers apart; when Saturn and Earth are at their most distant from each other, the distance between them is about 1.66 billion kilometers.
How long does it take to get to Saturn?
The duration of the flight to Saturn is determined by two factors: the route chosen for the mission and the speed of the spacecraft. For example, the Voyager 1 mission reached Saturn in 3 years and 2 months, and the Cassini spacecraft in 6 years and 9 months.
The most interesting satellite of Saturn
Titan is an unusual celestial body: it is the only moon in the entire solar system with a dense atmosphere similar to Earth, and the only place, with the exception of the Earth, on the surface of which there is liquid. Since the surface of Titan is very cold, there is no liquid water; however, the temperature is suitable for liquid methane and ethane. What's more, evidence has been found of an ocean beneath the moon's surface, where conditions are potentially habitable.
